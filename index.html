Bio

Lole Curik is a chameleon-like music producer, session musician, mixing engineer, and artist, recognized for his ability to adapt seamlessly across a wide range of styles and roles. After years of performing as an instrumentalist (drums, keyboards, and synths) with numerous artists throughout Argentina’s vibrant live scene, his career took a pivotal turn when he was awarded multiple scholarships from the Latin Grammy Cultural Foundation and Berklee College of Music. These opportunities enabled him to attend Berklee in Boston, where he earned a Bachelor's Degree in Independent Recording and Production, along with a Minor in Electronic Music Performance. During his time at Berklee, he immersed himself in sound design and in-the-box production, and was selected to join the prestigious Berklee Gender and Jazz Institute, studying under influential mentors and performing alongside emerging talents across the United States. Currently based in New York, Lole’s work is deeply rooted in record production, live performance, and sound design. Operating from his home studio, he collaborates with artists across genres including jazz, hip hop, indie, soul, and Hyper-Pop, contributing as a producer, instrumentalist, and mixing engineer. His experience spans recording sessions, live shows, and technical production, bridging the gap between acoustic musicianship and contemporary electronic soundscapes.

PERZona

PERZona is the solo project of Lorenzo Curik, a space where he fully expresses his artistic vision without the constraints of genre or conventional rules. Through this pseudonym, Lorenzo merges his diverse influences—R&B, neo-soul, jazz, sound design, and indie—into a unique, genre-blending sound. At the core of PERZona is collaboration and community. Each track represents a new world where Lorenzo works with different artists to create something authentic and meaningful. Themes of self-awareness, emotional introspection, relationships, and communication run throughout his work, with every project being produced, written, and composed by Lorenzo, alongside the featured artists who contribute their creative input. To date, Lorenzo has released his debut solo EP Arbitrarium in 2024, which explores a more sample-driven approach, blending organic recordings with electronic sounds. Later in 2024, he released And So I Go with Emma Where.In January 2025, Lorenzo released his first full-length album Lost Satellite, created with Austin Wilson and Phelt. The album delves into themes of self-awareness, community, and emotional connection, featuring a huge range of collaborations that contribute to its diverse, boundary-pushing sound.
